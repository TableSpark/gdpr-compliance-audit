# DSAR — filled example (access + erasure, small EU app)

*Illustrative output. A customer of a fictional EU app emails on 2026-07-10: "send me all the data
you have on me and delete my account." Article citations are real; dates are worked from the script.*

## Request summary & outcome per right
| Right | Article | Outcome | Reason |
|-------|---------|---------|--------|
| Access | Art. 15 | Need identity verification → then Fulfil | Must confirm requester before disclosing |
| Erasure | Art. 17 | Partially fulfil | Delete account data; retain invoice records under tax-law obligation (Art. 17(3)(b)) |

## Deadline
Received 2026-07-10 → base deadline **2026-08-10** (Art. 12(3)). No extension needed on these facts;
if the search proves complex, extension to **2026-10-10** is available with notice by 2026-08-10.

## Identity verification
Not yet established. Ask the requester to confirm from the account email on file (proportionate —
do not demand a passport scan) before releasing any data (Art. 12(6)).

## Per-right handling & exemptions
- **Access (Art. 15):** provide the data copy + supplementary info (purposes, recipients, retention,
  source, rights, right to complain to an SA). Redact any third-party personal data. — Partial until ID confirmed.
- **Erasure (Art. 17):** delete profile, usage, and marketing data. **Retain** billing/invoice
  records for the statutory tax-retention period — Art. 17(3)(b) legal obligation. Confirm scope to user. — Compliant with exception named.

## Recipient / processor notification (Art. 19)
Data was shared with the email/analytics processors. On erasure, instruct each to delete and confirm;
tell the user who they were if asked.

## Process maturity by area (0–4)
Intake 2 · Identity verification 2 · Data location 1 · Deadline tracking 3.

## What could not be assessed
Full list of processors holding the data (need the sub-processor register); whether any other
legal-retention obligation applies (need the retention schedule).
